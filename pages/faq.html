Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers about the Powering Tomorrow campaign, its learning pages, activities, podcast, artefacts, and family energy-saving habits.

What is Powering Tomorrow?

Powering Tomorrow is a student PSA campaign that helps families turn energy saving, climate awareness, and sustainable living into simple habits at home.

Who is the campaign mainly for?

The campaign speaks to families, with parents and carers guiding household choices and children building daily energy-saving routines.

What is the main goal of the campaign?

The goal is to reduce wasted energy, understand climate responsibility, and build sustainable habits that children can see, understand, and repeat.

Why does the campaign focus on the home?

The website explains that small actions at home can grow into lifelong habits because children notice and repeat what happens in normal family routines.

What does the PSA visual say?

The PSA visual uses the message "A Brighter Future Begins at Home" to inspire families to save energy, reduce costs, and embrace sustainable living.

Why are parents shown as important in the PSA?

The PSA visual presents parents as the biggest support in helping children understand energy-saving habits at home.

What small habits does the website recommend?

The site recommends switching off unused lights, unplugging chargers, turning off screens, using natural light, closing doors while heating or cooling is on, and noticing waste before it becomes normal.

How can parents teach children without making climate change scary?

The climate change page says children learn best when adults model useful routines, explain the reason in simple language, and keep the message calm and practical.

Why does household energy use matter?

Homes use electricity for lighting, heating, cooling, cooking, screens, chargers, and appliances. When electricity comes from fossil fuels, wasted energy can contribute to greenhouse gas emissions.

How can a family use an energy bill for learning?

The energy bill page suggests comparing the billing period, looking for what changed, and choosing one realistic habit to improve next month.

What should families look for on an energy bill?

Most bills show the billing period, total usage, supply charges, usage charges, and the amount owed. Usage is often measured in kilowatt-hours.

What is the difference between usage charges and supply charges?

A usage charge is linked to the electricity used, while a supply charge is usually a daily cost for being connected to the electricity network.

What does sustainable living mean on this website?

Sustainable living means making everyday choices that reduce waste, save resources, and support a healthier future through habits simple enough to repeat every day.

Does sustainable living mean buying new eco-friendly products?

No. The sustainable living page says it also means using what the family already has more carefully by repairing, reusing, sharing, and avoiding unnecessary purchases.

What is Captain Wattson's role?

Captain Wattson is the energy-saving superhero of Powering Tomorrow. He helps kids and families save energy, protect the planet, and become everyday eco-heroes.

What quick tips does Captain Wattson give?

Captain Wattson's page encourages families to turn lights off, unplug chargers, keep showers to five minutes, and use natural sunlight whenever possible.

What is Captain Wattson's Energy Quiz?

The quiz lets children choose an age group, answer climate questions, learn how to help the planet, and generate a certificate after completing the quiz.

Which quiz age groups are available?

The quiz includes groups for ages 6-8, 9-10, 11-13, and 14-15, each with 10 questions matched to that learning level.

What do the age-group infographics focus on?

The infographics give age-matched energy-saving messages, from small daily habits for younger children to smarter energy choices and overnight energy waste for older students.

What is Captain Wattson's DIY Energy Lab?

The DIY Energy Lab offers hands-on activities that help kids learn about renewable energy, sustainability, and smart everyday habits.

What does the DIY Solar Oven teach?

The solar oven activity uses sunlight, a shiny reflector, a dark surface, and trapped heat to show how natural warmth can do a job without plugging anything in.

What does the MacGyver Windmill teach?

The windmill activity uses moving air to spin blades, turn a dowel, and lift a cup of small weights while connecting the lesson to using airflow wisely at home.

What does the Reusable Lunch Bag activity teach?

The lunch bag activity turns old denim into something useful and helps children think about how one reusable item can replace throwaway ones.

What artefacts support the campaign?

The artefact page shows parent reminders such as a keychain and fridge magnet, plus Captain Wattson stickers for children.

What is the Powering Tomorrow podcast about?

The podcast episode "Everyday Energy: Smart Choices. Stronger Future." explores sustainability, energy use, everyday habits, systems, policy, renewable investment, and how parents and young people can influence change.
