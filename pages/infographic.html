What is an infographic?

A visual way to explain important information quickly.

An infographic combines short text, data, symbols, charts, and layout to make information easier to interpret. Instead of asking families to read a long explanation, it helps them notice the main idea, understand the pattern, and remember the action.

What infographics consist of

Clear content, strong layout, and simple design choices.

A good infographic needs a purpose, selected information, visual structure, and readable design. It should have a title, context, a body of key ideas, and a final takeaway or call to action.

Purpose and information

It starts with the point you want to make, the supporting facts, and the order readers should follow.

Visualisation

Charts, icons, symbols, timelines, maps, tables, or flowcharts can show ideas with less text.

Layout and hierarchy

The largest text should carry the main message, with headings and sections guiding the reader through the page.

Colour and consistency

Colours, fonts, spacing, lines, and arrows should make relationships clear while staying easy to read.
