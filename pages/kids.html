Family Energy Habits

Teaching children by age

Children learn best when energy-saving habits match their age and daily routine. Parents can keep the message simple, steady, and practical at every stage.

Ages 6-7

Children can learn the basics: turning off lights, closing doors when heating or cooling is on, and listening to simple reminders from parents.

Ages 9-10

Kids can start checking switches, unplugging chargers, and helping with small family routines before leaving a room or going to bed.

Ages 12-13

Pre-teens can understand that wasted electricity affects the family. Parents can involve them in conversations about bills, routines, and shared responsibility.

Ages 15-16

Teenagers can lead by example by managing their own devices, avoiding wasteful habits, and influencing younger siblings without being asked every time.
